## Edgardo Bonzi
### Research Interests

My research focuses on computational and experimental physics, particularly on the interaction of radiation with matter and the development of numerical modeling techniques for the analysis and simulation of physical processes.

My scientific activity spans several areas of radiation physics and computational physics, combining experimental work, theoretical analysis, and numerical simulation. A significant part of my work has been devoted to the study of X-ray spectroscopy and the determination of atomic parameters, including experimental measurements using synchrotron radiation.

I have also worked extensively on Monte Carlo simulations for radiation transport and radiation–matter interactions, with applications in radiation detection, dosimetry, and accelerator physics.

More recently, my research has expanded toward machine learning and artificial intelligence applied to physics problems, including neural network approaches for reconstructing radiation spectra and analyzing data from particle accelerators and radiation detectors.

Current research interests include:

- Radiation physics and gamma spectroscopy  
- Monte Carlo simulations of radiation transport  
- X-ray spectroscopy and atomic parameters  
- Density Functional Theory (DFT) and electronic structure calculations  
- Machine learning and neural networks applied to physics problems  
- Computational methods for radiation detection and accelerator physics

### Academic Activity

Former Full Professor at the Faculty of Mathematics, Astronomy, Physics and Computing (FaMAF), National University of Córdoba.

Throughout my academic career I have taught undergraduate and graduate courses in several areas of physics and scientific computing, including:

- Radiation physics  
- Acoustics and psychoacoustics  
- Monte Carlo methods  
- Scientific computing  
- Numerical methods  

Currently, I continue teaching as an independent lecturer in postgraduate programs, master's degrees, and specialized diploma courses, while remaining actively involved in scientific research at the Faculty of Mathematics, Astronomy, Physics and Computing (FaMAF), National University of Córdoba.
